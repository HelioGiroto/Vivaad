President-elect An -elect is a political candidate who has been elected to an
office but who has not yet been sworn in or officially taken office. These may
include an incoming president, senator, representative, governor and mayor.
Analogously, the term "designate" (e.g. Prime Minister-designate) is used in
systems without direct elections of executive politicians, such as in
parliamentary systems. Title. In the United States, a person elected to office
at the state or federal levels, and sometimes by custom at local levels, is
given the courtesy style of the "Honorable" (abbreviated to "Hon." or
"Hon'ble").
