Godhead in Christianity Godhead is a Middle English variant of the word "", and
denotes the Divine Nature or Substance ("Ousia") of the Christian God, or the
Trinity. Within some traditions such as Mormonism, the term is used as a
nontrinitarian substitute for the term "Trinity", denoting the Father, Son, and
Holy Spirit not as a Trinity, but as a unified council of separate beings.
